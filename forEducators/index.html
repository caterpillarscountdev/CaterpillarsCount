Taking it to the Classroom

A major goal of the Caterpillars Count! project is to provide a structured way for students and other interested individuals to learn more about the caterpillars and other insects that play a crucial role in our ecosystem.

One important avenue for learning is actually participating in Caterpillars Count! surveys. We hope participants gain an appreciation for the wild things that are easily overlooked, and an appreciation for the diversity of life all around us.

We are also very excited to promote the following additional learning experiences back in the classroom, whether or not your class is able to participate in field surveys.

Classroom Activities

The first three activities below were developed by Ryan Garland and Allen Hurlbert specifically for Caterpillars Count! The remaining activities focus on concepts related to Caterpillars Count!, but were developed by other organizations.

Arthropod Food Web

Suggested Grades: 6 - 9

Students explore the interconnectedness of many of the organisms they encounter when they are outside.

Phenology Across Trophic Levels

Suggested Grades: 6 - College

Students develop an understanding of the interconnectedness of the three trophic levels as they make connections between climate change and food abundances.

Inquiry-based learning using the Caterpillars Count! data visualization tools

Suggested Grades: 6 - College

Students explore Caterpillars Count! data using a variety of data visualization tools and guiding questions.

Phenology

Suggested Grades: 6 - 12

Students gain practice in graphing while learning the difference between weather and climate. Note: Only pages 9 - 14 are suggested for this activity

Vernal Pool Data Analysis

Suggested Grades: 4 - 6

Students enhance their graphing skills while also learning about vernal pools in the Harvard Forest.

How do Changes in Weather Affect Butterflies?

Suggested Grades: 9 - 12

Students practice statistical modeling while learning about the effects of weather on butterflies.

Do Caterpillars Eat Faster When It's Hot Or Cold?

Suggested Grades: 6 - 8

Students expand their graphing skills while learning about the effects of temperature on Manduca sexta caterpillars.

Weather and Climate Data Exploration

Suggested Grades: 6 - 12

Students explore the relationship between weather and climate by graphing weather temperature data and comparing with climate averages.

Part of the Pheno Mismatch project funded by the National Science Foundation
